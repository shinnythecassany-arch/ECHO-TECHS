About us

We are Echo-Techs, a group of students from St. John's Wort Integrated School Senior High School Level. The objective of our project is to develop an educational board game that demonstrates the detrimental impact of land-based activities, such as mining and construction, on our cultural heritage. Our project centers on the Petroglyphs of Angono-Binangonan, Rizal, a site featuring 127 figural carvings engraved on the walls of a shallow volcanic tuff cave. Through our interactive game, we seek to educate the public about the vulnerability of these Petroglyphs to various human activities and the importance of preserving them for future generations. Ultimately, this project aims to raise awareness about the significance of protecting our cultural heritage in an engaging and informative manner .

A.R.K Ancient Rock Keepers

The project focuses on the Angono–Binangonan Petroglyphs, the nearest archaeological site in our area, which we researched and visited to assess its current condition. Although no visible surface damage is evident despite the petroglyphs’ existence for thousands of years, our investigation revealed growing concerns surrounding the area. Compared to its earlier condition, the area is now surrounded by human-made activities. Aside from this, natural disasters and daily seismic vibrations also greatly contribute to the formation of micro-fractures, leading for micro-cracks to slightly open, which may eventually lead to the internal weakening of the rock art.

Our Response

In response, we developed an interactive board game designed to educate players about the factors that damage rock art and the actions needed to preserve it for future generations. Players encounter scenarios representing real threats, as well as preservation actions. This project also uses an AI-assisted interface that visually simulates the future condition of the petroglyphs based on different scenarios, such as neglect or proper preservation . Through gameplay, players are encouraged to work collaboratively, fostering awareness. By combining education with technology and fun, A.R.K. aims to ignite public interest in safeguarding cultural heritage while making learning engaging and meaningful.
